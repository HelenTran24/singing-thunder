Series 01 · TikTok & Instagram Reels

Stone Stories:
What Your Jewellery Knows

A slow, intimate, educational series that introduces wellness-oriented newcomers to the healing energies, cultural origins and spiritual significance of the stones and materials in Singing Thunder jewellery. Each episode centres one material — a stone, a metal, a colour — and unpacks its meaning across Afro-Indigenous traditions, its sourcing story, and how it can support a modern mindfulness practice.

Format
60–90 sec Reels / TikTok
Cadence

Primary Platform

Primary Audience
Wellness Seekers (Seg. B)
Episodes Planned
6 core + 2 seasonal

Episode Guide — Stone Stories

EP. 01 · LAUNCH

Turquoise: The Sky Stone

"This stone has been protecting people for 3,000 years. Here's what it can do for you."

Founder holds a turquoise cuff and traces its journey: where it was sourced, what it means in Indigenous Southwestern and West African traditions, and how wearing it near the throat supports authentic expression. Close-up: the stone's veins, the metal setting, the hands that made it.

turquoise throat chakra origin story mineral close-up

EP. 02

Rose Quartz: The Heart Stone

"It's not just pink. It's a 2,000-year-old love medicine."

A slow unboxing of a rose quartz pendant, filmed in golden morning light. Founder narrates: why this stone has appeared in Indigenous healing ceremonies across continents, what self-love actually looks like as a cultural practice, and how to activate your piece before wearing it.

rose quartz self-love ritual heart chakra morning light

EP. 03

Obsidian: The Shadow Stone

"This is the stone people are afraid of. They shouldn't be."

Dark, atmospheric filming. Founder at a dimly lit crafting bench with an obsidian ring. Explains its role as a protective stone in Mesoamerican and African spiritual traditions, its formation from volcanic fire, and why wearing it is an act of courage — not fear. Pairs beautifully with the autumn content calendar moment.

obsidian protection shadow work volcanic origin

EP. 04 · NIPD TIE-IN

Copper: The Healing Metal

"Before gold, there was copper. And it still heals."

Timed for the week of National Indigenous Peoples Day. Founder shows raw copper and a finished piece, describing copper's role in Indigenous medicine traditions across Turtle Island. Discusses how Singing Thunder uses recycled copper as an ethical choice. Educational, reverent, accessible.

copper NIPD recycled metals healing

EP. 05

Labradorite: The Intuition Stone

"When the light hits it right, you'll understand why they called it the Northern Lights stone."

Macro filming of labradorite's spectral flash — the labradorescence. Founder speaks about this stone's deep connection to Inuit mythology and its use as a stone of transformation and inner vision. Pairs with wellness audience interest in intuition, tarot and spiritual discernment.

labradorite intuition Inuit story macro lens

EP. 06 · SEASONAL

Garnet: The Winter Stone

"This stone is for the people who bloom in the dark."

Holiday season episode. Deep red garnet against a dark velvet background. Founder discusses garnet's role as a stone of vitality and regeneration across West African and Indigenous North American traditions — worn specifically in winter to retain warmth and strength. Perfect gifting narrative. Drives to the holiday collection.

garnet winter energy gift guide seasonal

Visual & Tone Direction

  • 🎥Filming style: slow, contemplative, macro-focused. Think jewellery editorial meets ASMR meets short documentary. No fast cuts.
  • 💡Lighting: always natural or candlelit. Soft warm tones, shadows allowed. No bright studio lighting.
  • 🎙️Voiceover: founder's own voice, calm and unhurried. No music over narration — let silences breathe. Soft ambient music under only.
  • 📍Set: crafting bench, outdoor nature settings, stone/wood textures. Organic, not studio-perfect.
  • ✍️Text overlay: minimal — only the stone name and one key phrase. Cormorant Garamond font style, cream on dark background.

Series CTA & Funnel Strategy

  • 🔗Every episode ends with: "The full story of this stone — and how it was made — is on our blog. Link in bio."
  • 💬Pin a comment on every episode inviting viewers to share which stone calls to them. Respond to every reply personally in the first 48 hours.
  • 📌Cross-post each episode to IG Reels with a Pinterest-optimized static image pulling a key quote as a promoted pin.
  • 📧Each episode should link to a blog post that has an email capture: "Get the monthly Stone Guide — a ritual for each new gem."
  • 🛒Feature the specific piece containing that stone in the video. The piece should be shoppable via link in bio or IG Shopping.

Series 02 · TikTok & Instagram Reels

Made With Meaning:
The Ethics of Beautiful Things

A bold, values-forward series for creative professionals who care about provenance, craft, and the story behind what they wear. Each episode pulls back the curtain on one aspect of Singing Thunder's process — from sourcing a recycled metal to designing a culturally inspired motif — and connects it to larger conversations about ethical consumption, cultural respect and the power of buying with intention.

Format
45–75 sec Reels / TikTok
Cadence
Bi-weekly (alternating S1)
Primary Platform
TikTok → Cross-post IG
Primary Audience
Value-Driven Creatives (Seg. A)
Episodes Planned
6 core + 2 seasonal

Episode Guide — Made With Meaning

EP. 01 · LAUNCH

Why I Only Use Recycled Metal

"The jewellery industry is one of the most environmentally destructive on earth. This is what we chose instead."

Founder at the bench, showing raw recycled silver next to a finished piece. Quick, factual, compelling. Covers: what metal mining does to land and communities, what recycled metal means in practice, and how choosing recycled is a form of Indigenous land stewardship. Tone: informed, passionate, not preachy.

recycled metals sustainability BTS ethical sourcing

EP. 02

The Symbol This Ring Carries

"Every Singing Thunder design means something specific. This one took 3 months to get right."

Founder draws a design motif — shows the sketch, the cultural reference material, the finished piece. Explains the Indigenous or Afro-diasporic symbol, what it represents, and the process of ensuring cultural accuracy and community permission. Speaks directly to creative professionals who understand the weight of design decisions.

design process cultural motif Afro-Indigenous symbolism

EP. 03

Lab-Grown Gems: The Truth No One Tells You

"People ask if lab-grown gems are 'fake.' Here's what the diamond industry doesn't want you to know."

Side-by-side comparison: a mined gem vs. a lab-grown equivalent. Founder breaks down: identical chemical composition, zero mining impact, fair pricing, and why lab-grown is an act of ethical alignment — not compromise. Uses the creative professional's lens: when you know what something costs to make, you choose differently.

lab-grown gem education ethical gems industry truth

EP. 04 · NIPD TIE-IN

Cultural Appreciation vs. Appropriation: Where's the Line?

"I get asked this every week. Here's the honest answer."

Founder speaks directly to camera — no props. Addresses the question that every non-Indigenous customer of Singing Thunder may wonder: can I wear this? What does it mean to wear Afro-Indigenous jewellery if I'm not from that culture? Answers with nuance: education, intention, source matters, and how buying from an Indigenous-owned brand is itself a form of allyship. Timed for NIPD week for maximum reach.

cultural respect NIPD conversation allyship

EP. 05

How Long Does It Actually Take to Make This?

"Customers ask why handmade jewellery costs what it costs. Let me show you."

Time-lapse of a single piece being made from raw material to finished jewellery. Founder narrates the time: 4 hours of forging, 2 hours of polishing, 1 hour of stone setting, 30 minutes of quality checking. Creative professionals understand this math immediately — it reframes cost as value and builds deep respect for the craft.

handmade process time-lapse craft value price transparency

EP. 06 · SEASONAL

Why We Launched a New Collection in November

"We didn't plan a Black Friday sale. We planned something more intentional than that."

New collection launch episode. Founder explains the cultural and seasonal significance behind the timing — harvest, gratitude, preparation for winter — and how the collection's themes connect to those cycles. Challenges the mindless consumerism of the holiday season and reframes the collection as intentional, story-driven gift-giving. Drives directly to the collection launch.

new collection intentional gifting seasonal cycles launch story

Visual & Tone Direction

  • 🎥Filming style: dynamic but grounded. Mix of talking head (direct-to-camera), B-roll crafting footage, and tight macro shots. Slightly faster pacing than Series 1.
  • 💡Lighting: bright, honest, functional. Daylight preferred. This series is about transparency — the aesthetic should feel like nothing is hidden.
  • 🎙️Voice: founder direct to camera for talking-head moments. Confident, informed, slightly provocative. Hooks should feel like an informed friend challenging an assumption.
  • 📍Set: workshop, studio, outdoor markets, crafting bench. Authentic spaces only — no artificial staging.
  • ✍️Text overlay: use bold caption-style text for key facts and statistics. Data-driven moments should be on-screen as text for accessibility and shareability.

Series CTA & Funnel Strategy

  • 🔗Every episode ends with: "The full sourcing/design story is in our blog. Link in bio." Drives organic traffic aligned with the +40% website goal.
  • 💬Pin a question comment on every episode: "What do you wish more brands were transparent about?" Engage every response for 48 hours post-publish.
  • 🤝After EP. 04 (Appreciation vs. Appropriation), DM every thoughtful commenter with a personal note and a discount code. These become brand ambassadors.
  • 📌EP. 02 and EP. 05 are designed for virality among creative professional communities — share directly with ethical fashion and design accounts for duet/stitch opportunities.
  • 🛒Each episode features one specific shoppable piece. The piece is shown being made, worn, and linked in bio. Drives direct conversion from educational content.

How the Two Series Work Together

Stone Stories (Series 1) attracts the wellness audience through emotion, ritual and spiritual resonance. Made With Meaning (Series 2) attracts the creative professional through transparency, craft and values alignment. Both series funnel to the same brand identity — Singing Thunder as ethical, culturally grounded, and deeply intentional — but speak each audience's language. Publishing them in alternating bi-weekly slots means Singing Thunder posts one high-quality series episode every single week, maintaining consistent presence without creator burnout. Both series share the anchor hashtag #WearYourStory, unifying the content under one brand movement.
