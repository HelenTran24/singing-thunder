Playfair Display — Display Typeface
High-contrast serif with dramatic thick-thin strokes. Used for all major headlines, brand name treatments, pullquotes, and product names. The tension between its bold uprights and delicate hairlines mirrors the tension between strength and delicacy in handmade jewellery. Available in Regular, Bold, Black and all Italic variants.

03 — Adinkra Symbols & Brand Language

Symbols thatspeak first.

Sankofa
"Go back and fetch it"
The wisdom of returning to your roots. Singing Thunder looks backward to move forward — drawing from ancestral knowledge to craft jewellery for today.
Gye Nyame
"Except for God"
The supremacy of a higher power. Singing Thunder's jewellery is made with reverence — acknowledging forces greater than commerce or trend.
Dwennimmen
"Ram's horns" · Strength & Humility
Power that bows in service. Singing Thunder is a strong brand built humbly — knowing that the craftsperson serves the stone, not the other way around.
Nyame Biribi Wo Soro
"God is in the heavens"
There is always hope. Each stone in Singing Thunder jewellery carries energy from beyond — mined from sky and earth, worn as a reminder that you are held.

Adinkra symbols from the Akan people of West Africa, used with reverence and cultural acknowledgement. All symbols are used with educational intent and respect for their living traditions.

05 — Brand Voice

How Singing Thunderspeaks.

01

Grounded & Reverent

Speaks from deep knowledge, never performance. Moves slowly and deliberately, like the crafting process itself. Silence is welcome here.

"This stone has been protecting people for 3,000 years. Here is what it can do for you."

02

Bold & Transparent

Doesn't shy away from hard conversations about the industry, about ethics, about cultural respect. Informed and direct — never preachy.

"The jewellery industry is one of the most environmentally destructive on earth. This is what we chose instead."

03

Warm & Inclusive

Every person — regardless of body, identity, or cultural background — is welcomed. Singing Thunder is a bridge, not a gate.

"Jewellery for every body. Every identity. Every love."

04

Poetic & Intentional

Captions, descriptions, and narration use careful, considered language. Metaphor is welcome. One strong sentence outperforms five average ones.

"This is the stone people are afraid of. They shouldn't be."

05

Storied & Educational

Every piece has a backstory. Every material has an origin. Every design carries meaning. The audience is treated as intelligent and curious.

"Before gold, there was copper. And it still heals."

06

Community-Rooted

Doesn't speak at its audience — speaks with them. Customers are invited into the creative process, the cultural learning, and the ongoing story.

"We didn't expect this much love. Thank you for celebrating with us."

"Rooted in the land.
Made for the living."

06 — Brand Do's & Don'ts

Protecting theintegrity.

✦   Always Do
✓Use warm, natural light — golden hour, candlelight, window light. Studio white is forbidden.
✓Contextualize every cultural symbol with an explanation — on-post or in the caption. Meaning before decoration.
✓Feature diverse models: all skin tones, body sizes, genders, and ages within the 25–46 range.
✓Move slowly. Let video breathe. Silence is as important as the soundtrack.
✓Credit and compensate every Indigenous collaborator and supplier visibly, by name.
✓Use the earth-dark palette as the base. Kente gold is the accent — not the wallpaper.
✓Embed beadwork patterns and Adinkra geometry as texture — always with cultural notes nearby.
✓Write captions that a curious new person could read and learn from without feeling excluded.
✕   Never Do
✕Use bright white studio backgrounds — they erase the warmth and cultural texture the brand lives in.
✕Use cultural symbols, patterns, or ceremonial references as mere decoration without context or credit.
✕Use generic sans-serif fonts — Inter, Roboto, Arial, Helvetica — in any brand-facing material.
✕Post trend-chasing content disconnected from Singing Thunder's cultural story and values.
✕Use hard-sell language or countdown urgency — it conflicts with the brand's intentional, ceremonial pace.
✕Post any image without a grounding caption. Every visual needs text, even one considered sentence.
✕Use neon accents, purple gradients, or trend-driven palettes that conflict with the earth-and-fire palette.
✕Treat Indigenous or Afro-diasporic culture as backdrop. It is the substance — not the aesthetic layer.
